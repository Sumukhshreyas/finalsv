Good

The PRD is a well-crafted, focused document for a clearly scoped product — a product showcase and enquiry-generation website for a Bangalore wholesale dealer. The strategic thesis ("rank on Google, generate enquiries") is coherent and every feature serves it. The no-e-commerce guardrail is stated with admirable force.

The main risk areas are: (1) two mega-FRs (FR-2 and FR-7) that bundle too many testable behaviours — these will cause friction during story decomposition; (2) the Assumptions Index doesn't round-trip with inline tags; and (3) several open questions should be resolved before the PRD can truly claim "final" status. None of these are structural defects — they're polish items that a 30-minute pass would fix.

Decision-readiness

Adequate

The PRD makes its biggest decision — "no e-commerce, enquiry only" — loudly and clearly, repeating it in the Vision (§1), Non-Goals (§5), and a dedicated guardrail (§8.4). The "reference HTML is sacred" constraint is also stated decisively. However, several decisions that matter for downstream are deferred to Open Questions rather than resolved: hosting platform, data management strategy, WhatsApp pre-fill behaviour, and domain name.

Medium

7 open questions with "final" status

§11
Several open questions (hosting, data management, WhatsApp pre-fill, domain name) are architectural decisions that should be resolved before the PRD can claim final status.
Fix: Resolve questions 1–3 and 6 with the product owner. Move the rest to a post-v1 backlog.
Low

Mode persistence mechanism unspecified

FR-1
FR-1 says mode "persists during the session" but doesn't specify how (sessionStorage, URL state, default value).
Fix: Specify: default is "automobile"; persists via URL path prefix or sessionStorage; resets on new session.

Substance over theater

Strong

The PRD is lean and every section carries weight. No persona theater — the three personas are distinct and map to real buyer types. No innovation theater — no novelty claims. No NFR theater — NFRs are baked into specific guardrails with concrete thresholds (LCP < 2.5s, touch targets ≥ 44px). The Glossary is tight and genuinely useful.

Strategic coherence

Strong

The PRD has a clear thesis: "generate enquiries and rank on Google for spare parts searches in Bangalore." Every feature serves this thesis. Success Metrics validate the thesis. The counter-metric (SM-C1, bounce rate) is genuinely insightful.

Low

SM-2 enquiry target may be miscalibrated

§7
20 WhatsApp/call enquiries per month may be too low or too high — current baseline is unstated.
Fix: Validate the target with the product owner against current offline enquiry volume.

Done-ness clarity

Adequate

Most FRs have testable consequences. The best examples are FR-1 (mode toggle), FR-19 (URL structure), and FR-7 (product detail). However, FR-2 and FR-7 bundle too many behaviours into single requirements, which will cause friction during story creation.

High

FR-2 bundles 6 distinct homepage sections into one requirement

§4.1
Each homepage section (hero, categories, brands, featured, advantages, contact band) is independently testable and could fail independently.
Fix: Split FR-2 into FR-2a through FR-2f, or add an explicit decomposition note for story creators.
High

FR-7 bundles 9+ display elements into one requirement

§4.4
The product detail page is the most complex page. Image, OEM chip, brand chip, compatibility grid, specs grid, description, gallery dots, favourite button, and back navigation are all packed into one FR.
Fix: Split into sub-requirements (FR-7a through FR-7d at minimum) or add a decomposition note.
Medium

FR-6 list view is underspecified

§4.3
The reference HTML only shows grid view fully implemented. List view layout is not specified.
Fix: Clarify whether list view is MVP scope or decorative. If in scope, specify the row layout.
Low

Search result prices are unexplained

FR-13 vs §5
FR-13 mentions "price indication" but §5 says "No pricing display (except indicative prices on search results)." The source/format of these prices is unspecified.
Fix: Add a note: prices in search results are hardcoded indicative values from the product data, not dynamic.

Scope honesty

Strong

The Non-Goals section does genuine work — it names specific things a reader might expect and explicitly closes them. The Out of Scope for MVP tags each item with a version target and includes reasoning. The assumptions are honest.

Medium

Assumptions not tagged inline

§12
7 assumptions are indexed in §12 but none appear as inline [ASSUMPTION: ...] tags at the point of inference in the feature descriptions.
Fix: Add inline tags in the relevant FR descriptions and verify the roundtrip.

Downstream usability

Adequate

The PRD is well-structured for downstream extraction. Glossary terms are used consistently. IDs are contiguous. Cross-references work. The IA table and data requirements table are directly usable. However, UJ persona labels don't exactly match §2.1 definitions.

Low

UJ persona labels don't match §2.1

§2.4
UJ-1 says "workshop mechanic" but §2.1 defines "Workshop Owner / Mechanic (Automobile)." Similar in UJ-2 and UJ-3.
Fix: Use exact persona labels from §2.1 in each UJ.
Low

Glossary term "OEM Number" used inconsistently

§3 vs §4
§3 defines "OEM Number" but features alternate between "OEM number", "OEM/spec number", and "OEM."
Fix: Standardize to "OEM Number" throughout, or add "OEM/Spec Number" as a combined glossary entry.

Shape fit

Strong

This is a product showcase / lead-gen website for a specific local business. The PRD correctly avoids over-formalizing: three personas, three user journeys, lean success metrics. The §8 Constraints section is an excellent shape choice for a design-constrained build. No over-engineering, no under-specification.

  • Glossary drift: "OEM Number" alternates with "OEM/spec number" and "OEM" across FRs.
  • Assumptions roundtrip: 7 entries in §12, 0 inline [ASSUMPTION] tags. Roundtrip broken.
  • UJ persona linkage: Persona names in UJs are approximate matches, not exact labels from §2.1.
  • Frontmatter note: §0 references product owner name that was edited; ensure consistency.
  • Cross-references: All FR→UJ and SM→FR references resolve correctly.
